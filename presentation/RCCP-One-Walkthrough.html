From the planner's seat — what I do, what I see, what I decide. The order is the same every month.

What I do
  • Open a new batch, drop all 6 files

  • Fix at source & re-upload anything BLOCKED
  • Publish the candidate baseline

3

Review · Exec Summary

Mid-month · RCCP meeting
What we see together
  • 12-month KPIs · lines at risk · site util · demand vs capacity
  • Per-plant Capacity vs Actuals charts (lime dot = new launch)
  • People fit — heads not hours · standard → effective with exception reasons
  • Planned downtime grid · portfolio changes · auto-commentary
→
4

Scenarios & sign-off

By month-end · approvers
What we decide
  • Pick a planning month · slide extra hours per line
  • See cost £ (extra litres × £0.12/L) and the crew implication per role
  • ELT / Site Ops approve · baseline locks
  • If the review changes inputs → Planning corrects → re-publishes a new candidate

The pitch in one sentence: one validated plan per month, one published baseline, one set of numbers the whole site debates — with the "people fit" and "planned downtime" answers visible up-front, not pieced together in spreadsheets.

What I'd like your feedback on

Four areas I care about — react to anything that feels off, missing, or over-engineered. Plain words are welcome.

Headcount & people fit core

  • Does the panel answer "have I got enough crew?" at a glance — without you doing maths?
  • Sheet 1 (line standard) + Sheet 2 (plant shared) + Sheet 3 (this cycle's exceptions) — workable for Manufacturing to maintain?
  • DATA NEEDED vs SHORTFALL distinction — does it land?
  • Heads, not hours — right unit for this question?

Line availability calendar

  • Planned maintenance & downtime sit on the line capacity calendar — feasible to maintain per cycle?
  • The Planned downtime grid (line × 12 months) — does it surface what you need for the meeting?
  • Partial-day losses captured via planned_hours — clear enough?

Capacity & scenarios decisions

  • 12-month outlook + planning-month spotlight — right horizon for sign-off?
  • Per-line hour slider with £0.12/L flat cost and crew implications — actionable for OT / extra shift calls?
  • What's missing to make "approve this plan" obvious?

Governance & trust process

  • One published baseline per cycle + corrections re-published — practical for the cadence we run?
  • Audit trail — every batch, every file version kept. Enough for sign-off / disputes?
  • Anything you'd remove? Anything you'd want before we ship?
✎

Format I'd like: walk through the live app on each section together · 5 minutes per panel · capture anything that feels wrong, unclear, or missing. I'll fold the feedback into the next iteration and circle back before we go wider.
